ABOUT ME

Hi there! I am an evolutionary biologist, who uses computer simulations and mathematics to explore the evolution of complex biological systems. I am studying how evolution and second-order selection shape phenotypes and populations, with its share of surprising and counter-intuitive effects.

I am using a broad range of modeling approaches, from mathematical modeling to individual-based and multi-level simulations. Depending on their complexity, I usually consider models as experimental systems (Peck, 2004) — I conduct in silico evolution experiments in a computer as one would do in the real world (Hindré et al., 2012).

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Balázs Papp laboratory. Before that, I joined INRIA (France) from May, 2019 to August, 2020 as a postdoctoral fellow. I defended my PhD in November, 2017 at INSA de Lyon (France), within the project-team INRIA-Beagle.
